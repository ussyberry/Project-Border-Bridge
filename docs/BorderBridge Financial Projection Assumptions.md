# BorderBridge Financial Projection Assumptions

## Overview

This document outlines the key assumptions used to develop the 3-month financial projections for BorderBridge's AI-powered immigration guidance platform. These assumptions are based on the business model, pricing structure, and 30-day launch plan, with realistic estimates for user acquisition, conversion, and operating expenses.

## Sensitivity Analysis Factors

The financial projections should consider sensitivity analysis around these key variables:

1. **User Acquisition Rate**: ±20% variation in new user registration
2. **Conversion Rate**: ±25% variation in trial-to-paid conversion
3. **Customer Churn**: ±30% variation in monthly churn rate
4. **Marketing Efficiency**: ±15% variation in customer acquisition cost
5. **Pricing Mix**: Shift in distribution across subscription tiers

## Notes on Assumptions

1. **Conservative Approach**: These projections take a moderately conservative approach to early growth, assuming gradual improvement in key metrics.

2. **Seasonal Factors**: No significant seasonal variations are expected in the first 3 months.

3. **Competitive Landscape**: Assumptions account for current competitive environment without major new market entrants.

4. **Regulatory Environment**: No significant immigration policy changes are assumed during this period.

5. **Technology Performance**: Assumptions presume no major technical issues or downtime affecting user acquisition or retention.

6. **Marketing Effectiveness**: Projections assume marketing campaigns perform within industry benchmarks for SaaS platforms.

7. **Expert Availability**: Sufficient immigration experts are assumed to be available to meet demand for the Voyager plan.

These assumptions will be refined based on actual performance data as it becomes available during the launch and early operations.
